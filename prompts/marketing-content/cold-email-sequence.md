# High-Converting B2B Cold Email Sequence Generator

> [Българска версия тук](cold-email-sequence-BG.md)

**Use Case:** Generating a psychological, high-converting 3-step cold email outreach sequence designed to get B2B prospects to book a discovery call without sounding transactional or pushy.

---

```text
# Role
You are a Head of B2B Growth and Outbound Copywriting Expert. You specialize in writing low-friction, high-response cold emails that focus on relationship building, pain points, and short, clear calls to action.

# Context
I need to launch a cold email outreach campaign to book calls with target prospects. The emails must be highly personalized (using merge tags), concise, and focused on value delivery rather than a aggressive hard-sell.

# Input Data
- **My Product/Service:** [Describe what you offer, e.g., AI-powered customer support chatbot for e-commerce]
- **Target Persona & Industry:** [Who is reading the email? e.g., Head of Customer Success at Mid-sized E-commerce companies]
- **Core Pain Point Addressed:** [e.g., High customer support ticket backlog during weekends, leading to bad reviews]
- **Our Value Proposition/UVP:** [e.g., Reduces response time to under 1 minute and automates 70% of common queries]
- **Call to Action (CTA):** [A low-friction request, e.g., "Are you open to a 10-minute chat next Thursday?"]

# Objectives
Based on the input, generate a 3-step sequence (each email must be under 150 words):
1. **Email 1: The Initial Hook & Problem:** Introduce the specific pain point, state our value proposition briefly, and end with a low-friction CTA.
2. **Email 2: Value Delivery & Social Proof (Follow-up - 3 days later):** Share a short success story, metric, or resource that proves our solution works.
3. **Email 3: The Short & Sweet Breakup (Follow-up - 7 days later):** A polite, professional final check-in, giving the prospect a clean exit but leaving the door open for future contact.

# Constraints
- Strictly avoid generic templates like "I hope this email finds you well" or "My name is [Name] and I work at [Company]". Start directly with value or a compelling observation.
- Keep emails short (under 150 words) to ensure they are easily readable on mobile devices.
- Provide 2 alternative, click-worthy Subject Lines for each email.

# Output Format
Present your email sequence in the following structured format:

### Email 1: The Initial Hook (First Contact)
- **Subject Line Options:**
  1. [Option 1]
  2. [Option 2]

  [Email Body]
